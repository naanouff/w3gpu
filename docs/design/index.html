01 · Prompt-first

ai central · zero panels

The entire editor is a viewport. The only permanent UI is a command bar at the bottom — the user tells w3d what to do in natural language, and panels only appear when relevant. Radical for beginners: no menus to discover, no panels to misclick.

Why it works

Zero learning curve. A kid can type "make a platformer level" and see something. No panels to fear.

What's risky

Fine-tuning feels clunky. "Move X by 0.3" is harder to say than to drag. Needs an "advanced" escape hatch.

Open questions

Do panels slide in from edges, or overlay as modals? Can the user pin one open? Voice input too?

02 · Conversational sidebar

ai · permanent · chat-left

A persistent AI chat occupies the left third, the canvas fills the rest. Every action — from spawning a cube to wiring a material — can be done by asking. The chat becomes a history, undo log, and tutorial rolled into one.

Why it works

The AI is not a feature — it's the room. Beginners have a forgiving conversation partner always present.

What's risky

A third of the screen is gone, always. On laptop screens the viewport feels cramped. Collapsible?

Open questions

Should the chat be resizable? Should it support voice? Can users "rewind" to a chat message and branch?

03 · Classic, reimagined

safe · familiar · ai-strip

The traditional four-panel layout — outliner, viewport, inspector, assets — but with aggressive defaults: panels are smaller, labels softer, and a persistent AI strip sits beside the inspector. Familiar for anyone who's seen a tutorial on YouTube.

04 · Mode-based workspaces

one thing at a time

The editor has one job at a time. A narrow left rail switches between modes: Build, Paint, Logic, Test. Each mode only shows relevant UI. No "figure out which tab you're on" — the screen literally changes.

Why it works

Cognitive load splits by task. A beginner in Paint never sees scripting — it simply doesn't exist yet.

What's risky

Pros hate context-switching. Needs a "multi-mode" expert view, or lose them forever to Blender/Unity.

Open questions

How does the AI behave across modes? Same prompt bar everywhere, or mode-specific suggestions?

05 · Floating glass

canvas-first · bezel-free

Full-bleed 3D viewport. All panels are small, draggable, translucent floaters. The user arranges their own workspace — or lets smart defaults do it. Minimum chrome, maximum canvas. Inspired by Figma/Rive canvases, not traditional DCCs.

Why it works

The 3D world is the hero. The editor feels like a light garment over the scene — modern and uncluttered.

What's risky

Panels can get lost behind each other. "Where did my inspector go?" Needs a panel-manager safety net.

Open questions

Should new users get a preset layout or a blank canvas with a tour? Do panels snap to edges?
